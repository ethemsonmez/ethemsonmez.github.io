The Onion Router

Why would someone who has nothing to hide wants some privacy? Edward Snowden's answer seems accurate, he says "Arguing that you don't care about the right to privacy because you have nothing to hide is no different than saying you don't care about free speech because you have nothing to say." The Browser of the Tor Projecta (501(c)(3)) (which I will refer as TOR) will help you to protect your identity. Actually, this is not the first step you should take if you want to change some online habits, but I will be focusing on Tor in this section. I think it's pretty cool. At the beginning, like early 2000's, main idea was developed by US Army. Yikes. But they realised to use Tor, they should make it public. So they released the code under a free licence. You get the idea, yes even your government has spies and they probably communicate over TOR network. EFF was one of the very early funders of TOR Project.

But hey, isn't this the place where all the bad stuff going on? Like, people hiring hitmans, livestreaming red rooms and all the illegal stuff? NO. Well, technically, criminals will have access to TOR as well but let's talk scientific: "We show that only a small fraction of users globally (∼6.7%) likely use Tor for malicious purposes on an average day. However, this proportion clusters unevenly across countries, with more potentially malicious Tor users in “free” countries (∼7.8%) than in “not free” regimes (∼4.8%)."* We can see that, what TOR provides is outweighs the criminal usage. People just wants to be Free. Tor is mostly being used by journalists, activists and people like you and me who just want to see the real internet.

Download and use Tor, or run a relay!

*Jardine, Eric; Lindner, Andrew M.; Owenson, Gareth (15 December 2020) https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7749358

Julian Assange

Activist, journalist, terrorist? It's a shame that we usually see this trio together. This is also the case for Assange itself as well. Born in 1971, Australia, Assange lived like a nomad with his parents, they traveled a lot. He started hacking when he was 16 (let's have a moment here to say that hacking and malicius attacks are different than each other.) after, few years, in 1994, later federal police charged him with 31 hacking and related crimes and he pleaded guilty to 24 charges in 1996. He studied programming, mathematics and physics but but did not completed the school. Starting with 90's he worked for public good, worked with police to prosecute individuals responsible for child pornograhpy distribution, worked with open source patches and so on.

Than WikiLeaks came in 2006. Assange was in advisory board and he was the editor-in-chief. In 3 years period they released some leaks, and attracted few people gained some enemies. Everything has gone more serious with leaks from Chelsea Manning (US Army whistleblower). In 2010, WikiLeaks published Collateral Murder which shows US Army killing 18 civilians including 2 journalists on duty. Than the "Iraq War Logs" came, total of 391,832 field records directly from the U.S. Army. Those leaks was following thousands of documents and followed by thousands of leaks.

It's no surprise that US was against him now. US started an investigation in 2010 to prosecute him under the Espionage Act which is like a 100 year old law. In 2012, Assange applied for political asylum from Ecuador and he stayed at Ecuadorian embassy in London. In 2019, Ecuador's president said that Assange violated the terms of asylum, just after some leaks connecting Monero to some corruption scandal. What a surprise. So Assange got arrested in London. Recently, The United States government has won an appeal at Britain’s High Court over the extradition of WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange. That's why there is a section of Assange in this site. There is a high chance of him to extradict a country which has no fair trial system. Snowden is avoiding the US for this reason as well.

Don't shoot the messenger.
